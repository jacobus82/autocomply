Why your bio matters more than you think
A potential client checks your bio before deciding whether to contact you. It's often the only thing standing between a lead and a client.
🎯
Lead with what you do, not who you are
Most people start with their name and title. Start with the value instead. "I help Gauteng homeowners get electrical work done right first time" lands harder than "Master Electrician with 12 years experience".
🔑
Specificity beats impressive-sounding
"500+ installations, 4.9 stars on Google" is more credible than "passionate and dedicated professional". Real numbers, real outcomes. Prospective clients are evaluating risk — give them evidence.
📍
Location matters for local businesses
If you serve a specific area, say it in every bio. "Electrical contractor" competes with the whole country. "Johannesburg electrical contractor" captures your actual market.
🔄
Update bios every 6 months
Most people set their bio once and forget it. As your business grows, your bio should reflect it. A stale bio with an old service focus or missing credentials is a silent conversion killer.
