The rules of responding to Google reviews
When potential clients see a review, they're also evaluating your response. Every public reply is a sales message.
✓ Do this
Respond to every review — positive and negative. Businesses that respond to reviews rank higher on Google and convert better.
✕ Never do this
Never argue publicly — even if the reviewer is wrong. Potential clients reading your response will judge you by how you handle conflict, not who's right.
✓ Do this
Move the conversation offline for negative reviews. Give a phone number or email. Resolving it privately often leads to the reviewer updating or removing the review.
✕ Never do this
Never use the same template for positive reviews. "Thanks for the 5 stars!" on every review looks automated and insincere. Reference something specific.
✓ Do this
Respond within 24–48 hours — especially to negative reviews. A quick, professional response signals that you're attentive and care about your clients.
✕ Never do this
Never admit legal liability in a negative review response. "We're sorry this happened and want to make it right" is fine. "You're right, it was our fault" can be used against you.
