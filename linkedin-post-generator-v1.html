Why LinkedIn posts get ignored — and how to fix it
Four principles that separate posts that get engagement from posts that disappear.
1
The hook is everything
LinkedIn shows only the first 1–2 lines before "see more". If the hook doesn't make someone stop scrolling, nothing else matters. Lead with the most interesting thing — not the context.
2
White space is your friend
Short paragraphs (1–3 lines) with space between them are far more readable in the LinkedIn feed than dense blocks. People skim first, read second. Make skimming easy.
3
One idea per post
The best LinkedIn posts make one point very well. Not three points reasonably well. Identify the single most important thing you want readers to take away — build everything around that.
4
Consistency beats virality
One post a week for 6 months beats one viral post and nothing. The LinkedIn algorithm rewards consistent creators with improved organic reach. Build a publishing habit, not a posting event.
