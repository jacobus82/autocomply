Zero-rated ≠ exempt. On zero-rated supplies you charge 0% VAT but can still claim input VAT back. On exempt supplies (financial services, residential rent, public transport) you charge no VAT and can't claim input VAT.
How to use this calculator
Three everyday scenarios — the tool handles all of them the same way.
Adding VAT to a quote
You quoted a client R 10 000 ex-VAT. Type 10 000 into the Excluding field. You'll see the VAT amount (R 1 500) and the total invoice value (R 11 500) immediately.
Extracting VAT from a total
A customer paid R 1 150 inclusive. Type 1 150 into the Including field. You'll see the ex-VAT portion (R 1 000) and the VAT you must declare (R 150).
Working with zero-rated items
If you supply zero-rated goods — bread, maize meal, fresh produce, exports — click the 0% chip. VAT amount drops to R 0 while the ex-VAT and inclusive values remain the same.
Historical or custom rates
For historical calculations (14% pre-April 2018) or hypothetical scenarios, type any value into the Custom chip. The calculation updates instantly at your chosen rate.
Approaching the R 2.3m threshold?

Once your turnover crosses R 2.3 million in a 12-month period, VAT registration becomes compulsory. We can guide you through SARS registration, eFiling setup, and the admin that follows.
