WhatsApp broadcast rules that get more replies
The difference between a broadcast that gets blocked and one that gets replies is technique — not luck.
👤
First name matters more than anything
A message that starts with someone's name gets read. One that starts "Dear Customer" gets ignored. Always personalise with at least a first name — even if you send 200 messages.
🎯
One ask, one action
Don't ask someone to call, WhatsApp, visit your website AND follow you. One CTA only — reply to this message, or call this number. Make it effortless to respond.
⏱
Timing is a lever
SA WA broadcast best windows: Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10am (before daily grind sets in) or 6–8pm (after work). Avoid Mondays before 9am and Fridays after 3pm.
📋
Only broadcast to opted-in contacts
POPIA requires consent for marketing messages. Only send to contacts who have given consent — customers, previous enquiries, or opted-in lists. Cold broadcasts risk being reported as spam and your account blocked.
🔁
Max twice a month to any list
More frequent than that and reply rates drop, unsubscribes increase, and spam reports follow. Quality over frequency. One great broadcast beats four forgettable ones.
🚫
No links in the first message
Links in broadcast messages reduce deliverability and read rates. Get a reply first — then send the link in the private conversation that follows. Conversation beats cold click-through.
